## THE ENDING EXPLAINED

**Final Scene**: 2045, Remainder school

Elena (75) teaches 30 children about systems and purpose.

Yuki (9), daughter of Japanese engineer who left Tokyo's automated zones, raises her hand.

**Yuki**: "I have an idea." (About scaling windmills for power)

**Elena**: "Tell us."

**And she does.**

**We never hear what Yuki says.** That's intentional.

**Why?**
- The point isn't the specific idea
- It's that she HAS ideas
- That she believes ideas matter
- That the future is still being shaped by humans with agency
- The reader's imagination completes it
- What would YOU build?

**The bet paid off**: Not through defeating automated systems, but through people choosing meaning over efficiency when given the option.

**Both models coexist**: Automated zones (some people prefer security) and Remainder zones (most people choose meaning). No triumphalism. Just options.

**The future is unwritten**: And that's enough.
